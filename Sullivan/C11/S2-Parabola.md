Find the vertex, focus, and directrix

__48__) $x^2 + 6x - 4y + 1 = 0$

__Sln__: $x^2 + 6x - 4y + 1 = 0 \implies 4y - 1 = x^2 + 6x$; now add 9 to both sides to complete the square on the right, yielding $4y + 8 = (x+3)^2 \implies 4(y+2) = (x+3)^2$ which matches the form $4a(y-k) = (x-h)^2$ with the identifications $a=1, h = -3, k = -2 \implies \boxed{\text{vertex} = (-3,-2)}$, focus $= (-3, -2+1)=\boxed{(-3,-1)}$, and the directrix is $\boxed{y=-3} (=-2-1)$.
